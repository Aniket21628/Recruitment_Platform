RULES & GUIDELINES

All students who expect to graduate from the Institute by the end of the academic year 2023-2024 and are seeking employment may register for campus placements with this office. Placement Registration is for ONE ACADEMIC YEAR ONLY.

Registration for all programmes will be done during the month of August.

MS & PhD scholars placement will happen round the year. Their registration will be valid for one year only. They can register by attaching a certificate (NOC) & Abstract in the prescribed format available in the Placement website.

Inter-disciplinary (ID) Research Scholars will be permitted to upload resumes for both the departments as approved at the time of admission to the program. The NoC for ID Research Scholars must be signed by the guide from both the departments.

Campus placement is a facility provided for the students. Registration is not compulsory. Students not interested in placement are advised not to register for placement.

Backlogs:

Students having backlog of 4 or more courses are not permitted to register for placement. Such students are advised to clear the backlogs and then register after the July-November end semester examinations. However, extended students can register in case of non-completion of course/project requirements in their last two semesters.

Re-Registration:

Students who have lost the job for genuine reasons will be allowed to re-register on a case to case basis.

Students who have graduated in 2024, but not placed due to various genuine reasons, may be permitted to re-register with the approval of the concerned authorities. They will be permitted to appear for placement only from Day 1 or later, as approved by the Board of Placements.

Each registered student will be given a Placement Registration (PR) Number to access the placement notices, company announcements and to upload resumes.

Students are advised to prepare their resume with care. Resume has to be uploaded on the placements website.

The format of resume is available in the placement website. This format is to be followed strictly.

Details of projects done, papers presented, internships undergone and any other achievements should be highlighted in the resume.

The information given in the resume will be verified by the office. Any discrepancies found in the resume at the time of verification will result in debarring of the student from the placement for the rest of the academic year.

At the time of appearing for the interview, students are advised to carry multiple copies of their resume, photograph and other supporting documents.

Once a student has uploaded the resume, it will be verified by a staff member of the placement office. The student can make necessary corrections, if any, based on the queries raised by the staff member.

The resume will be released to the company concerned, only after the verification process is completed.

Students are advised to avoid using the photographs of them wearing T-shirts, casual wear, etc. They should be formally dressed in the photographs.

Notices of the PPT will be published in the Placement website well in advance.

Students should occupy the venue 15-mintues before the scheduled start of the PPT.

Students interested in a particular company, can attend the PPT of that company. Attendance for PPT is not compulsory.

Queries and clarifications, if any, may be clarified by the students with the company representatives during PPT.

A copy of the resume can be handed over to the company executive during the PPT, if required.

Students are advised to carefully read the Job Announcement Form (JAF) before applying to a company. Students should apply only if they are eligible and interested.

The placement office will publish the list of companies, along with the job profile, CTC and other details. Students are advised to go through these details and apply only to the companies which meet their expectations.

Students are advised to avoid picking and choosing the companies and the roles. They should apply generally for all the companies where they meet the eligibility criteria.

The placement office will inform the students of the deadline for submitting applications. Students are expected to meet the deadlines and apply on time.

Resumes once submitted for a particular company cannot be withdrawn at a later stage.

It is the responsibility of the student to check announcements/notices/ shortlisted names etc. in the notice boards/website.

Students are expected to be punctual and present themselves for the interview process as per the notifications.

A student who applies and gets shortlisted is bound to go through the entire selection process unless rejected midway by the company.

Any student who withdraws deliberately in the midst of a selection process will be disqualified from placement for the rest of the academic year.

Late comers for the Aptitude Test / Group Discussion / Interview may not be allowed to appear for the selection process.

Students should carry all the necessary documents like resume, photo, certificates, etc.

Students should be formally dressed whenever they participate in any interaction with a company.

Misbehaviour / indiscipline / irregularity will not be tolerated and students involved in such cases will be debarred from placements for the rest of the academic year.

The copy of the offer letter is required to be submitted in the placement office.

The company will send the offer letters to the placement office. The placement office will handover the offer letters to the concerned students.

Offers received from the companies need to be accepted or rejected by the student within 3 days of receipt of the offer.

All offers of a particular company will be announced at the end of the selection process of that company only.

In case, students decide not to join the company, they should inform the placement office in writing along with the reason.

Once an offer is accepted, the student is out of the campus placement process and will not be allowed to apply to any other company.

However, a student can apply to companies, which come with a dream offer provided that the CTC is 1.5 times higher than the first offer.

A student can apply for a maximum of 2 dream offers.

If a student receives an offer from a dream company, the earlier offer automatically stands released and the student will be out of campus placements.

A student with a single offer has the option to withdraw the offer and re-register for placement for a dream company. The job offer can be withdrawn with the approval of the Professor-in-Charge, Placements and with prior written consent from the company. Such a student is required to submit an undertaking duly signed by the parent/guardian.
